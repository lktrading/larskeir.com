1 Rushing into a trade

If we are making many trades one after another, in retrospect, we may see that our entries were not well thought out. This is especially true if we are gripped by emotions. You may find yourself exiting a very good trade early, or a bad one too late. This is why many traders report their biggest losses after their big wins.

In the last article, we talked about confidence. Does the speed of your decision-making make a difference in that confidence? The answer is no.

The research shows that you can be equally confident whether you are slow or fast in making a decision. That is to say, you can be slow and still be uncertain (or certain). You can also be fast in making a decision and be uncertain (or certain).
